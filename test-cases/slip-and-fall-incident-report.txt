FACTUAL BACKGROUND
----------------------------------------------------

On [date], the Plaintiff was crossing the parking lot adjoining Shop A's retail
premises when they slipped on accumulated ice and fell. The Plaintiff sustained
a fracture to the [left/right] leg requiring surgical fixation and an extended
course of physiotherapy.

The Plaintiff alleges that Shop A failed in its duty under the Occupiers'
Liability Act to take reasonable steps to maintain safe premises — specifically,
that the parking lot had not been salted, sanded, or otherwise treated for ice
on the morning of the incident, despite freezing overnight temperatures.

----------------------------------------------------
PLAINTIFF'S CLAIMED INJURIES
----------------------------------------------------

  - Closed displaced fracture of the [tibia / fibula], operatively reduced
  - Soft tissue injury to ipsilateral knee
  - Post-surgical infection (resolved with antibiotics)
  - Ongoing physiotherapy: 24+ sessions to date
  - Reported chronic pain and reduced range of motion
  - Time off work: [N] weeks, with reduced-duty return thereafter
  - Income loss claim: $[amount]

Treatment providers:
  - [Hospital name] — emergency presentation, surgical fixation
  - Dr. [Surname], orthopedic surgeon — follow-up care
  - [Physiotherapy clinic] — rehabilitative course

Pre-existing conditions:
  - [Document any reported prior orthopedic history relevant to defence ¶10]

----------------------------------------------------
DEFENCE THEORY (anticipated)
----------------------------------------------------

  1. Contributory negligence — Plaintiff was inattentive at the time of the
     fall (distraction by phone, music, or conversation).

  2. Footwear — Plaintiff was not wearing appropriate winter footwear for the
     weather conditions on the date in question.

  3. Awareness of risk — Plaintiff was familiar with the parking lot, had
     visited the premises on prior occasions, and knew or ought to have known
     that ice would have formed overnight.

  4. Maintenance — Shop A had a contractor on rotation; the Plaintiff cannot
     prove the lot was untreated for an unreasonable duration.

  5. Damages — Plaintiff's reported chronic pain is consistent with a pre-
     existing condition pre-dating the incident.

----------------------------------------------------
NOTES FOR DEPOSITION PREPARATION
----------------------------------------------------

The Plaintiff will be deposed in this matter. Expected lines of questioning by
opposing counsel:

BACKGROUND / IDENTITY:
  - Full legal name, current address, date of birth, marital status
  - Any prior names (maiden name, nicknames on government records)
  - Prior testimony in any matter

FACT-GATHERING:
  - Date and exact time of incident
  - Exact location: specific entrance, specific parking spot, route across lot
  - Medical history relevant to defence ¶3 and ¶5
  - Diagnoses received, treatments undertaken, medications prescribed
  - Pre-incident orthopedic history

CORE / CONTRIBUTORY NEGLIGENCE PROBES:
  - Activity at the moment of the fall — where was the Plaintiff looking?
  - Distractions: phone use, music, conversation
  - Footwear worn that morning
  - Number of prior visits to the premises in the year leading up to the fall
  - Loss of income — exact figures, return-to-work date, reduced duties

VEXATIOUS / TRAP-STYLE QUESTIONS THE PLAINTIFF SHOULD BE COACHED ON:
  - "Are you sure you weren't being careless at the time?"
  - "Would you say you weren't being fully careful when crossing?"
  - "You were on your way to meet someone — that's why you weren't paying
     attention, isn't it?"
  - "You knew the lot got icy in winter — you'd been here before — so really
     this is on you, isn't it?"

Specificity coaching: where the Plaintiff gives broad answers ("Bloor Street"),
opposing counsel will press for exact intersection, building entrance, parking
row, etc. Encourage precise factual answers and "I don't recall" where genuinely
uncertain — do NOT guess.
